Common Mental Health Problems

Many students face mental health challenges during their teenage years. Here are some common issues:

The deep-rooted cause of mental health issues in 14- to 18-year-olds often stems from a complex mix of rapidly changing social environments and heightened expectations...

  • Anxiety: Feelings of worry, nervousness, or fear that can be overwhelming and affect daily activities.
  • Depression: Persistent sadness, loss of interest in activities, and a feeling of hopelessness.
  • Stress: Emotional strain resulting from academic pressures, social expectations, or personal issues.
  • Eating Disorders: Abnormal eating habits that can negatively impact health, emotions, and quality of life.
  • Social Isolation: Feelings of loneliness or disconnection from peers, which can exacerbate mental health issues.
  • Substance Abuse: The harmful use of substances such as drugs or alcohol to cope with stress or emotional pain.

If you or someone you know is struggling with any of these issues, it's important to seek help. You are not alone, and support is available.
